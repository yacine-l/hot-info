## The Weight of History: Understanding the Market Town Status

To truly appreciate Bojanov, one must first understand its classification: the *městys* or market town. This designation is critical, as it signifies a community that transcended the status of a mere village (*ves*) but never quite achieved the administrative or economic magnitude of a city (*město*).

Historically, the right to hold regular markets was a highly coveted privilege, often granted by the Bohemian monarchy or powerful local nobility. This privilege transformed a settlement into a local hub for commerce, administration, and social exchange. For Bojanov, this status cemented its importance along regional trade routes, likely connecting centers like Chrudim and Pardubice with the agricultural hinterlands.
